About Me

Hello! I'm Sakdipat “Jaf” Kongkaeo, a graduate student in Atmospheric & Oceanic Sciences. My academic interests center on climate variability, large-scale ocean–atmosphere interactions, and ENSO predictability using advanced linear inverse modeling methods.

Outside of research, I enjoy photography, baking, and learning guitar and bass. These hobbies help me stay creative and curious—qualities I also bring into my scientific work.

Research

My current work focuses on ENSO predictability using Cyclostationary Linear Inverse Modeling (CSLIM). I’m particularly interested in how seasonally varying dynamics and noise forcing shape the growth, decay, and predictability limits of ENSO events.

This includes projects on:

  • Seasonal noise forcing and ENSO variability
  • Floquet modes and time-dependent stability
  • SST/SSH EOF reconstructions for LIM/CSLIM systems
  • Forecast spread analysis and stochastic optimals
